Complete Guide to Sideloading Applications on iOS

What is sideloading?

Sideloading is the process of installing programs onto a device through sources other than the ones given by the device manufacturer.
In this case, Apple only allows app installations exclusively from the App Store. However, there are ways to get around such limitations.
Apple requires all apps to be digitally signed, AKA, 'approved' before launching on your device.
This is different from other platforms such as android, which allows users to install any application, regardless of being signed or 'approved'.

Why would I want to sideload apps?

Apple has strict limitations and guidelines regarding the things they allow in the Appstore.
If a developer doesn’t comply with these guidelines they will either be rejected from the App Store or get their app taken down later upon review.
Also, applications are often removed from the Appstore by the developers themselves for various reasons.
These reasons can include no longer supporting the app, ownership change,
and commonly, to introduce a new version of the app with a subscription based payment model.
With sideloading, you can install any app of your choosing.

So how do I sideload applications on my iOS device?

Applications can be signed in two ways.

1. Use of personal Apple ID (Free)
2. Use of external App Signing services (Some free / Some paid)


What is a signing service?

A signing service is an application or website that provides applications for the user to install.
These applications include but are not limited to jailbreaking apps, tweaked apps, tweaked games, paid games, etc.
Signing services work on all devices on all iOS versions.
No computer is required.
